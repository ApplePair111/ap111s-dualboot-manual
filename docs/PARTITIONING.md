# Hi there! Welcome to the Partitioning Guide!

## Step 1. Think
First, think about how big your partitions are gonna be. 

I chose about 195 GB for Linux and about 989 GB for Windows. (C drive + D drive)

I reccomend at least 500 to 600 GB for Windows and at least 150 to 200 GB for Linux. 
You can switch these up if you want, giving Linux more space.

## Step 2. Shrink

Now, shrink the volume of a big partition IF YOU HAVE SPACE.

MAKE SURE YOU DON'T TOUCH ANY EFI BOOT PARTITION.

If you have a big unallocated space, good! Move forward!
